Úrsula Corberó - Tokio

Úrsula Corberó Delgado is a Spanish actress. She became known in Spain for playing Ruth Gómez in the teen drama series Física o Química, Margarita de Austria in the historical fiction series Isabel, and Marta in the comedy film Girl's Night Out.

Winning awards

She debuted as Maria in the television series Mirall Trencat in 2002. She appeared as Sara in Ventdelplà in 2005–06, and in series Cuenta atrás in 2007. In 2008, she portrayed Manuela Portillo in series El Internado and began working on the Antena 3 television series Física o química until 2010.n March 2015, she starred as Nadia in Perdiendo el Norte alongside the actors Blanca Suarez and Yon Gonzalez.This same year, she received the Ciudad de Alicante Award, an award created by the Alicante Film Festival to encourage Spanish young talents.In 2015, she also landed her first main role in a movie in Cómo sobrevivir a una despedida and confirmed she's one of the best comical actresses of Spain.In 2017, Corberó landed a main role in the heist television series La Casa de Papel (Money Heist). She played Tokyo, the narrator of the story, a runaway robber who is scouted by the Professor to participate in his plan.
